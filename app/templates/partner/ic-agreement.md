# Independent Contractor Sales Partner Agreement

This Independent Contractor Sales Partner Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into as of __________***, 20*** (“Effective Date”) by and between:

**LeadCareAI / ____________________________**, a ____________________________ with its principal address at ____________________________ (“Company”),

and

**____________________________**, located at ____________________________ (“Partner”).

Company and Partner may be referred to individually as a “Party” and together as the “Parties.”

## 1. Purpose

Company provides lead response, missed-call text-back, AI-assisted SMS lead qualification, lead dashboard, follow-up, notification, reporting, and related services for local businesses under the LeadCareAI brand.

Partner desires to introduce, refer, and/or sell Company’s services to prospective business customers in exchange for commissions, subject to the terms of this Agreement and any applicable Commission Plan Addendum.

## 2. Independent Contractor Relationship

Partner is an independent contractor and is not an employee, agent, legal representative, franchisee, partner in a legal partnership, joint venturer, or co-owner of Company.

Partner is not entitled to salary, hourly wages, overtime, unemployment insurance, workers’ compensation, employee benefits, health insurance, retirement benefits, paid leave, or tax withholding from Company.

Partner is solely responsible for all taxes, expenses, insurance, licenses, registrations, and costs arising from Partner’s activities under this Agreement.

Partner controls the manner, means, time, and place of performing services, subject only to Company’s brand, compliance, customer protection, and approved marketing requirements.

Nothing in this Agreement gives Partner authority to bind Company, sign contracts on Company’s behalf, collect money on Company’s behalf unless expressly authorized in writing, modify Company pricing, promise services Company does not offer, or make guarantees to customers.

## 3. Partner Duties

Partner may perform the following activities:

1. Identify potential business customers.
2. Introduce Company’s services to prospects.
3. Share Company-approved sales materials, demo links, referral links, and scripts.
4. Book product demos or sales conversations.
5. Refer prospects through assigned referral links or referral codes.
6. Assist prospects with understanding the basic value of Company’s services.
7. Comply with Company’s marketing, privacy, brand, and sales rules.

Partner must conduct all activities lawfully, professionally, and honestly.

## 4. No Exclusive Territory

Unless Company signs a separate written territory agreement, Partner receives no exclusive territory, exclusive market, exclusive customer category, or exclusive right to sell Company services.

Company may sell directly, assign other partners, advertise, market, or accept customers from any area or industry.

## 5. Commission Eligibility

Partner may earn commissions only when all of the following are true:

1. The customer is a real business customer.
2. The customer signs up for Company’s services.
3. The customer’s payment is successfully received and confirmed.
4. The customer is properly attributed to Partner through Company’s referral tracking, referral code, admin assignment, or written approval by Company.
5. Partner is active and in good standing at the time the commission is earned.
6. The payment has not been refunded, disputed, charged back, reversed, or cancelled.
7. The commission is approved under the applicable Commission Plan Addendum.

No commission is earned from recruiting other partners alone.

No commission is owed for free trials, test accounts, unpaid accounts, fake accounts, internal accounts, refunded payments, disputed payments, cancelled customers, or customers not properly attributed to Partner.

## 6. Commission Plan

Commissions are governed by a separate **Commission Plan Addendum**, which may include activation bonuses, recurring commissions, retention bonuses, team overrides, payout timing, refund rules, dispute rules, clawback rules, and other compensation details.

Unless a different written Commission Plan Addendum applies, the starting compensation model is:

1. **Activation Bonus:** $100 per activated paid customer.
2. **Monthly Recurring Commission:** $25 per month for each active paid customer attributed to Partner.
3. **Retention Bonus:** $100 after the customer remains paid and active for six consecutive paid months.
4. **Team Override:** If enabled by Company in writing, $10 per month on active customers sold by directly sponsored partners only.

Company may modify the Commission Plan for future customers or future commissions by providing written notice to Partner. Changes will not affect commissions already approved for payment unless a refund, dispute, chargeback, reversal, fraud issue, or policy violation applies.

## 7. Commission Status, Approval, and Payment

Company may track commissions using the following statuses:

1. **Pending:** Commission has been recorded but is not yet payable.
2. **Approved:** Commission has passed review and is approved for payout.
3. **Paid:** Commission has been paid.
4. **Cancelled:** Commission is not payable.
5. **Reversed:** Commission was previously approved but later became invalid.
6. **Clawback:** Commission was paid but must be offset against future amounts owed.

Commissions are not payable until approved by Company.

Company may delay approval or payment during a refund, cancellation, fraud, dispute, chargeback, compliance, attribution, or customer-status review.

Unless otherwise stated in writing, approved commissions are paid monthly or on another schedule determined by Company.

Partner must provide all required tax and payout information before receiving payment.

## 8. Refunds, Disputes, Chargebacks, and Clawbacks

If a customer payment is refunded, disputed, charged back, reversed, cancelled, or determined to be fraudulent:

1. Pending commissions may be cancelled.
2. Approved but unpaid commissions may be reversed.
3. Paid commissions may be clawed back or offset against future commissions.
4. Company may suspend future payments until the issue is resolved.

Partner agrees that commissions are based on actual collected and retained customer revenue, not merely customer signup, checkout attempt, invoice creation, or verbal commitment.

## 9. Taxes and Required Forms

Partner is responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes arising from compensation paid under this Agreement.

Before receiving payments, Partner must provide Company with a completed IRS Form W-9 or other required tax documentation.

Company may issue IRS Form 1099-NEC or other applicable tax forms if required by law.

Company will not withhold payroll taxes, income taxes, Social Security, Medicare, unemployment taxes, or similar amounts unless required by law.

## 10. Approved Marketing and Sales Conduct

Partner must use only truthful, professional, and approved claims when discussing Company.

Partner may not:

1. Make false, misleading, exaggerated, or unapproved claims.
2. Guarantee income, customer results, lead volume, business growth, appointments, revenue, or profit.
3. State or imply that Company is an answering service, emergency service, law firm, medical provider, insurance carrier, financial advisor, or licensed trade service unless expressly approved in writing.
4. Promise pricing, service availability, appointment times, emergency response, refunds, discounts, or custom features without written authorization.
5. Use fake testimonials, misleading screenshots, false scarcity, fake case studies, or fabricated customer results.
6. Make earnings claims to potential partners unless Company has approved the exact written claim and supporting materials.
7. Spam businesses or violate telemarketing, email, SMS, privacy, consumer protection, or advertising laws.
8. Represent themselves as a Company employee.
9. Use Company’s name, logo, brand, or materials in a misleading way.
10. Create websites, social accounts, ads, domains, or marketing materials using Company’s brand without written approval.

Company may require Partner to remove, revise, or stop using any marketing material at any time.

## 11. No Authority to Bind Company

Partner has no authority to:

1. Sign contracts for Company.
2. Accept payments for Company unless expressly authorized.
3. Modify Company terms, pricing, cancellation rules, refund rules, or services.
4. Promise technical capabilities not currently offered.
5. Enter into agreements with vendors or customers on Company’s behalf.
6. Make legal, medical, financial, insurance, emergency, or compliance promises on Company’s behalf.

Any unauthorized promise or representation by Partner is Partner’s sole responsibility.

## 12. Customer Ownership and Attribution

All customers, prospects, leads, referral records, business accounts, account data, platform data, conversations, payment records, and customer relationships belong to Company.

Partner may receive commission credit for attributed customers under the Commission Plan, but Partner does not own the customer account or customer relationship.

Company has sole discretion to determine attribution in cases of duplicate referrals, existing customer relationships, multiple partners, incomplete tracking, fraud concerns, or disputed referral credit.

## 13. Confidentiality

Partner may receive access to confidential information, including customer information, pricing, sales materials, scripts, product roadmap, software screens, business strategy, partner data, commission data, technical information, login credentials, and non-public Company information.

Partner agrees not to disclose, copy, misuse, sell, transfer, or exploit confidential information except as necessary to perform authorized activities under this Agreement.

This confidentiality obligation continues after termination of this Agreement.

## 14. Data Protection and Privacy

Partner may not misuse customer, prospect, lead, or platform data.

Partner must:

1. Protect login credentials.
2. Access only accounts and information Partner is authorized to access.
3. Not export, sell, scrape, publish, or share customer data.
4. Not share screenshots containing personal information unless approved.
5. Not contact customer leads except as authorized by Company.
6. Notify Company promptly of suspected unauthorized access, lost credentials, data exposure, or security incident.

Company may immediately suspend Partner’s access if Company suspects misuse of data or unauthorized access.

## 15. Intellectual Property and Brand Use

Company owns all rights to its name, logo, brand, software, website, platform, sales materials, scripts, templates, designs, documentation, processes, workflows, and intellectual property.

Company grants Partner a limited, revocable, non-exclusive, non-transferable permission to use approved Company materials solely to promote Company services during the term of this Agreement.

Partner may not copy, modify, reverse engineer, resell, white-label, or create derivative products from Company’s platform, materials, or business model without written permission.

Upon termination, Partner must stop using Company brand assets, materials, referral links, demo access, and confidential information.

## 16. Non-Solicitation and Customer Protection

During the term of this Agreement and for twelve months after termination, Partner may not knowingly:

1. Solicit Company customers to cancel Company services and move to a competing service.
2. Use Company customer lists or platform data to sell competing services.
3. Interfere with Company customer relationships.
4. Misrepresent Company to customers, partners, or prospects.
5. Divert Company customers to a competing product using confidential information obtained through Company.

This section does not prevent Partner from continuing general business activities that do not misuse Company confidential information or customer relationships.

## 17. Compliance With Laws

Partner must comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, including laws related to advertising, telemarketing, email, SMS marketing, privacy, unfair competition, business opportunity claims, earnings claims, consumer protection, and independent contractor activity.

Partner is responsible for obtaining any business registrations, licenses, permits, or insurance required for Partner’s own business activities.

## 18. Expenses

Partner is responsible for all expenses incurred in performing services unless Company approves reimbursement in writing before the expense is incurred.

Company is not responsible for Partner’s phone, internet, travel, advertising, software, equipment, taxes, insurance, or business expenses unless expressly agreed in writing.

## 19. Platform Access

Company may provide Partner with access to a partner dashboard, referral links, sales resources, demo materials, or other tools.

Company may suspend, limit, or revoke access at any time for security, compliance, fraud prevention, inactivity, suspected misuse, termination, or business reasons.

Partner must not share login credentials or allow unauthorized users to access Company systems.

## 20. Term and Termination

This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues until terminated.

Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time, with or without cause, by written notice.

Company may terminate or suspend Partner immediately for:

1. Fraud or suspected fraud.
2. Misleading claims.
3. Data misuse.
4. Brand misuse.
5. Customer complaints.
6. Violation of law.
7. Violation of this Agreement.
8. Misrepresentation of Company services.
9. Unauthorized collection of payments.
10. Damage to Company reputation.

Upon termination, Partner must stop representing Company, stop using Company materials, stop using referral links, and return or delete confidential information upon request.

## 21. Effect of Termination on Commissions

Termination does not automatically cancel approved unpaid commissions unless Partner violated this Agreement or the applicable Commission Plan.

Company may withhold, cancel, reverse, or offset commissions after termination if:

1. The customer refunds, disputes, cancels, or charges back payment.
2. Partner violated this Agreement.
3. Partner made false or misleading claims.
4. Partner misused Company data or brand assets.
5. The commission resulted from fraud, fake accounts, unauthorized activity, or improper attribution.
6. Company determines the commission is not valid under the Commission Plan.

Recurring commissions after termination are payable only if allowed by the current Commission Plan Addendum and only while the customer remains active, paid, and not refunded or disputed.

## 22. No Guaranteed Income

Company does not guarantee that Partner will earn any amount of money.

Actual earnings depend on Partner’s own effort, sales activity, expenses, customer retention, customer payment status, market conditions, and compliance with Company policies.

Partner acknowledges that no income, revenue, commission, sales volume, customer count, or business result is guaranteed.

3. Partner’s promises or representations not approved by Company.
4. Partner’s tax obligations.
5. Partner’s misuse of customer data or confidential information.
6. Partner’s negligence, misconduct, fraud, or violation of law.

## 24. Limitation of Liability

Company’s total liability to Partner under this Agreement will not exceed the unpaid approved commissions owed to Partner for valid customer payments received during the three months before the event giving rise to the claim.
