SMS Program Terms

Last updated: May 27, 2026

This page describes how SMS messaging works when you interact with a local business that uses {{ settings.app_name }} for missed-call follow-up and lead management. It also sets baseline rules for businesses that send messages through our platform (A2P 10DLC / carrier compliance).

1. Program description

When you call a business and they do not answer, the business may use {{ settings.app_name }} to send you an automated text message asking how they can help. If you reply, the business may send follow-up messages to qualify your request (for example, service needed, location, or urgency) and arrange a callback. Message content is sent on behalf of the business you contacted, not for unrelated marketing lists.

2. Consent (opt-in)

By calling the business’s published phone number and/or replying to an initial text, you consent to receive service-related SMS from that business about your inquiry. Businesses must not use {{ settings.app_name }} to send texts without a lawful basis and documented consent where required by law.

3. Message frequency

Message frequency varies based on your conversation. Typically you may receive a small number of messages related to a single inquiry. Additional messages may be sent if you continue the conversation or request follow-up.

4. Opt-out

You can opt out at any time by replying STOP to the business’s text thread. After you opt out, you may receive one final message confirming your opt-out. For help, reply HELP or contact the business directly.

5. Costs

Message and data rates may apply depending on your mobile carrier plan.

6. Supported carriers

Wireless carriers are not liable for delayed or undelivered messages. Delivery is subject to your carrier and network availability.

7. Privacy

Information collected through SMS is handled as described in our Privacy Policy. Businesses are responsible for their own privacy disclosures to their customers.

8. Businesses using {{ settings.app_name }}

Business customers agree that they will:

  • Provide accurate business identity and contact information for registration;
  • Honor STOP and HELP requests promptly;
  • Send only messages related to the customer’s inquiry or existing relationship;
  • Maintain records of consent where required;
  • Not share or resell phone numbers obtained through the Service.

9. Sample messages

Examples of messages you may receive (wording may vary by business):

  • “Sorry we missed your call — this is [Business Name]. How can we help you today? Reply STOP to opt out.”
  • “Thanks — we received your message and will follow up shortly.”
  • “What service do you need help with? Reply STOP to opt out.”
